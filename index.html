Yumeng Hou

Assistant Professor @ National University of Singapore | [NUS Profile]

I research computational archiving and living archives, working at the intersection of computation, visualisation, digital curation, and cultural big data. Through both experimental and applied research, I investigate how digital and data-science methods can enhance the representation, transmission, and transformation of intangible cultural heritage, particularly through somatic and performative experience. My curatorial practice mobilises art-tech augmented interfaces to extend the fourth wall, moving fluidly across spaces of exhibiting, playing, and performing.

My work is shaped by an interdisciplinary trajectory. I received my PhD in Digital Humanities and Computational Museology from EPFL, Switzerland. I hold an MSc in Computer Science (EPFL), a BEng in Digital Media Technology (ZJU), and professional experience in the cloud computing industry. Before joining NUS, I was a Global Research Assistant Professor at the School of Creative Media, City University of Hong Kong.

Research

Research Projects

Computational Embodied Archives (2025–2029)

Current debates on the documentation, transmission, and transformation of intangible heritage highlight the complexities of engaging with embodied traditions. This project seeks to navigate these challenges by leveraging computational analysis and algorithmic curation.

Remapping martial arts history with multimodal AI (2025–2028)

This project seeks to enrich the intellectual and cultural history of Chinese martial arts through the development of a domain-specific compound AI system, along with curated datasets.

Central African Art – Invocation of an Unseen World (2024–2025)

A mixed-media journey immersed in Central African heritage, jointly curated by Hing Chao, Henry Lu, Amanda Wang Youlin, and Yumeng Hou (new media). Presented at the Indra and Harry Banga Gallery, May 9 - Sept 28, 2025.

HKMALA 2.0 (2025– )

Extending beyond the original MoCap-centred HKMALA, this project employs a computational archiving approach to re-render the HKMALA data as algorithmic yet semantically rich data assets. It will produce embeddings and knowledge graphs accessible through agentic interfaces.

HKMALA: Hong Kong Martial Arts Living Archive (2012– )

HKMALA represents the first comprehensive digital initiative to archive and annotate the living kung fu traditions using advanced motion-capture and audiovisual recording technologies.

MAETP (2022–2024)

MAETP – Transforming Lingnan Martial Arts as a Movement and Performing Art through Digital Pedagogy, Interpretation and Performance.
The project pioneers a systematic movement programme recognising the deep links between Chinese martial arts and performing arts.

EncodingActs (2020–2024)

EncodingActs: Modeling, Representing and Transmitting Embodied Knowledge in Traditional Martial Arts investigates how computational methods can facilitate the transmission of multifaceted knowledge within intangible cultural heritage in the context of traditional martial arts.

Computational Interoperability For Intangible and Tangible Cultural Heritage (2021–2022)

CROSSINGS aims to develop an ontological model that integrates data on intangible heritage by combining aspects of haptics, pose, and motion with meaningful traits of traditions and folklore.

Archery Rites: Remaking Confucian Rites (2020–2022)

Ritual archery has a long and vital history in Chinese civilization. This chapter examines the current reenactment and digital reconstruction of the specific tradition of the Archery Rites embedded in the Confucian tradition, ritual practice and cosmology of li.
